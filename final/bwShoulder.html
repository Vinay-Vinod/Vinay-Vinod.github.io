BODYWEIGHT SHOUDLER
Incline Pushup 1. Assume an incline plank position with your arms fully extended and your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
2. Lower your body towards the ground by bending your elbows while keeping your core engaged and your back straight. Lower until your chest is just above the ground.
3. Push yourself back up to the starting position by straightening your arms and returning to the plank position.
Plank to Downward Dog 1.Start in a high plank position with your hands directly under your shoulders and your feet hip-width apart.
2. Press your hips up and back, lifting your tailbone towards the ceiling and straightening your legs into a downward dog position.
3. Return to the high plank position by lowering your hips and returning to the starting position.
